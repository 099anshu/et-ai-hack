Title: RBI Advisory on Banking and Digital Payment Fraud

RBI (Reserve Bank of India) has issued multiple advisories on digital fraud. This document summarizes key guidance for citizens.

RBI'S CORE RULES — WHAT YOUR BANK WILL NEVER DO

1. Your bank will NEVER ask for your PIN, password, CVV, or OTP over phone, SMS, or email.
2. Your bank will NEVER ask you to install a remote access app (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, etc.).
3. Your bank will NEVER ask you to transfer money to verify your account or to "protect" your funds.
4. Your bank's official website always has .bank.in or .com domain — verify before entering credentials.
5. Your bank will NEVER threaten to block your account if you don't act within minutes.

RECOGNIZING FAKE BANK COMMUNICATIONS

Fraudulent SMS: Contains a link that leads to a phishing site. Official bank SMS never contains a URL asking for login.
Fraudulent Email: Uses domains like sbi-alerts@gmail.com or hdfc-bank@outlook.com. Official emails use @sbi.co.in, @hdfcbank.com, etc.
Fraudulent Call: Caller knows your name and last 4 digits of your card (obtained from data breaches). They use this to appear legitimate.

SECURING YOUR BANK ACCOUNT

Internet Banking:
- Use a strong, unique password — do not use the same password on other sites.
- Always log out after using net banking.
- Enable OTP-based transaction confirmation.
- Register your mobile number for transaction alerts.
- Regularly check your account statement for unauthorized transactions.

Debit/Credit Cards:
- Memorize your PIN — never write it on the card or store it with the card.
- Cover the keypad when entering PIN at ATMs and POS terminals.
- Inspect ATMs for skimming devices (extra keypad overlay or camera near keypad slot).
- Report a lost or stolen card to the bank immediately (cards can be hot-listed 24x7).
- Enable international transactions only when traveling abroad; disable when back.

ATM Safety:
- Prefer ATMs in well-lit, monitored locations.
- Be aware of strangers offering to "help" at ATMs — a common distraction technique.
- If your card gets stuck in the ATM, call the bank's helpline immediately — do not leave.
- Change your ATM PIN every 6 months.

REPORTING UNAUTHORIZED TRANSACTIONS

If you notice an unauthorized transaction:
1. Call your bank's 24x7 helpline immediately (number on the back of your card).
2. Ask them to place a hold on your account.
3. Call 1930 within the same hour.
4. Document: transaction amount, date/time, merchant or recipient name, reference/UTR number.
5. File at cybercrime.gov.in.

RBI TIMELINE FOR COMPLAINT RESOLUTION:
- Bank must acknowledge your fraud complaint within 3 working days.
- Provisional credit for zero-liability cases must be given within 10 working days.
- Final resolution within 90 days.

BANKING OMBUDSMAN
If your bank fails to resolve the complaint satisfactorily within 30 days:
- File with the Banking Ombudsman at RBI: cms.rbi.org.in
- Toll-free: 14440
- The Ombudsman can award compensation up to Rs.20 lakh for deficiency in service.
