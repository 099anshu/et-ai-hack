Title: Cyber Fraud Emergency Checklist

If you have just been defrauded online, act within the first 60 minutes — banks
can freeze fraudulent transactions if notified quickly.

IMMEDIATE STEPS
---------------
1. Call 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline) — available 24x7.
2. Call your bank's 24x7 helpline and ask them to place a HOLD on your account.
3. Do NOT transfer any additional money, even if the fraudster says it will "reverse" the first transaction.
4. Take screenshots of all messages, call logs, UPI transaction IDs, and payment receipts.
5. Note down the fraudster's phone number, UPI ID, bank account number, and website URL if available.

FILING A COMPLAINT
------------------
- Online: cybercrime.gov.in → Report Financial Fraud
- You will need: transaction date/time, amount, your bank account, recipient account/UPI ID
- Keep the complaint acknowledgement number for follow-up.
- For amounts above Rs.1 lakh also file an FIR at the nearest police station.

ACCOUNT SECURITY
----------------
- Change your net banking password and UPI PIN immediately.
- Enable transaction alerts via SMS for all accounts.
- Check your CIBIL report at cibil.com within 30 days for any fraudulent loan applications.
- If your Aadhaar was shared, lock biometrics at myAadhaar.uidai.gov.in.

WHAT NOT TO DO
--------------
- Do not accept a "refund" offer from the same fraudster — it is a second scam.
- Do not install any app the fraudster recommends.
- Do not share OTP or bank PIN with anyone, including people who call claiming to be police.

RECOVERY CHANCES
----------------
- Reported within 1 hour: ~60% chance of transaction reversal
- Reported within 24 hours: ~30% chance
- Reported after 7 days: very low, but complaint is still important for FIR and insurance claims
