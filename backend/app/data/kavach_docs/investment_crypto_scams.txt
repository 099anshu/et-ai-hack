Title: Investment Fraud and Cryptocurrency Scams in India

SCALE OF THE PROBLEM

Investment fraud is the highest-value cybercrime category in India. SEBI and RBI together received over 2.5 lakh investment fraud complaints in 2023-24. Individual losses range from Rs.50,000 to Rs.5 crore. The scams are run by organized syndicates, often from abroad.

HOW INVESTMENT SCAMS BEGIN

The typical entry point is a WhatsApp message: "Join our Premium Stock Advisory Group — 100% verified tips from SEBI expert." Or a Telegram link: "Earn 3% daily on crypto — our AI trading bot never loses." Or a social media ad: "Invest Rs.10,000 in 7 days get Rs.30,000 — limited slots."

Once inside the group, you see "members" posting fake profit screenshots. The group admin (the fraudster) builds credibility over days or weeks before asking for investment.

TYPES OF INVESTMENT SCAMS

1. FAKE STOCK TRADING PLATFORMS
Fraudsters create fake stock trading apps/websites that look exactly like real platforms (Zerodha, Groww, Angel One). Your "portfolio" grows on screen — but the money is never in a real market. When you try to withdraw, they demand tax clearance fees, SEBI approval fees, or RBI verification charges. Every payment generates another demand.

2. CRYPTOCURRENCY PUMP-AND-DUMP
Fraudsters talk up a specific cryptocurrency in groups, driving up its price. They sell their holdings at the peak. The price crashes. Newcomers who bought at peak suffer losses. Variations include "pig butchering" where a romantic relationship is built online before the investment pitch.

3. PONZI / CHIT FUND SCAMS
Promises of guaranteed monthly returns (3-15%). Early investors receive payouts funded by newer investors' capital. When new recruitment slows, the scheme collapses. Recent India examples: Gainbitcoin, GainBTC, various MLM crypto schemes.

4. FAKE IPO SCAMS
Messages claiming insider access to upcoming IPOs at a discount. Fraudsters collect money but never actually invest. No SEBI-regulated IPO is ever sold through WhatsApp or private messages.

5. COMMODITY AND FOREX SCAMS
Fake commodity trading groups claiming expertise in gold, silver, crude oil, or forex. Require you to open accounts on unregulated platforms. When profits accumulate, withdrawal is blocked.

WARNING SIGNS (RED FLAGS)

- Guaranteed returns above 10% per year (equity markets don't guarantee returns).
- Pressure to invest immediately or lose the "opportunity."
- Investment platform is not SEBI-registered (check: sebi.gov.in).
- Promoter is not SEBI-registered Research Analyst or Investment Advisor.
- Requires payment to "unlock" or "withdraw" your profits.
- Only communicates via WhatsApp/Telegram — no physical office, no phone number.
- Claims AI bots guarantee profits — no algorithm guarantees profit in live markets.

HOW TO VERIFY

Before investing, check:
1. Is the platform SEBI-registered? Search on sebi.gov.in/sebiweb/other/OtherAction.do?doRecognisedFpi=yes
2. Is the mutual fund AMFI-registered? Check at amfiindia.com
3. Is the broker SEBI and NSE/BSE registered? Check on nseindia.com or bseindia.com
4. Is the investment advisor SEBI-registered? Check at sebi.gov.in/investors/advisors
5. Google the company name + "SEBI action" or "RBI warning"

WHAT TO DO IF SCAMMED

1. Do not pay any more money — any "fee to withdraw" is another scam.
2. Call 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline).
3. File at cybercrime.gov.in — include platform URL, amount paid, UPI IDs used.
4. File a complaint with SEBI at scores.sebi.gov.in if the fraud involves investment products.
5. Report to local Economic Offenses Wing (EOW) of police.
6. Report the app to Play Store / App Store — fraudulent apps get taken down within 24-48 hours of mass reports.
