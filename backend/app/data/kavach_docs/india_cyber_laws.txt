Title: Indian Cyber Crime Laws and Legal Rights of Victims

LEGAL FRAMEWORK

India has several laws under which cybercrime is prosecuted:

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY ACT, 2000 (amended 2008)
- Section 43: Unauthorized access to computer systems — compensation up to Rs.1 crore.
- Section 66: Computer-related offenses (hacking) — imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine up to Rs.5 lakh.
- Section 66C: Identity theft (using someone's password/digital signature dishonestly) — imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs.1 lakh.
- Section 66D: Cheating by personation using computer resources — imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs.1 lakh.
- Section 66E: Violation of privacy (publishing private images) — imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine up to Rs.2 lakh.
- Section 67: Publishing obscene material in electronic form — imprisonment up to 5 years and fine.
- Section 72: Breach of confidentiality and privacy — imprisonment up to 2 years and/or fine up to Rs.1 lakh.

INDIAN PENAL CODE (IPC) SECTIONS
- Section 420: Cheating — imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.
- Section 468: Forgery for purpose of cheating — imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.
- Section 419: Punishment for cheating by personation — imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine.
- Section 489A–489E: Counterfeiting currency notes and possession of counterfeit currency — imprisonment up to life imprisonment for manufacturing, up to 10 years for possession/circulation.

WHERE TO FILE A COMPLAINT

Option 1 — Online (fastest):
Website: cybercrime.gov.in
Open 24x7. Select "Report Financial Fraud" for UPI/banking fraud. You will need: amount, date/time, recipient UPI ID or bank account, your bank details.

Option 2 — Phone:
1930 — National Cyber Crime Helpline (24x7)
The helpline can flag accounts and initiate holds with banks immediately.

Option 3 — Local Police Station:
File an FIR under relevant IPC/IT Act sections. For amounts above Rs.1 lakh, it is recommended to file an FIR in addition to the online complaint.

Option 4 — Adjudicating Officer:
Each state has an IT Act Adjudicating Officer for civil complaints under Section 43. Claims up to Rs.5 crore can be filed here without going to court.

YOUR RIGHTS AS A VICTIM

1. Right to an FIR: Police cannot refuse to register an FIR for cybercrime. If refused, file a complaint to the Superintendent of Police or approach the Magistrate.
2. Right to anonymity: For sensitive cases (online harassment, intimate image abuse), complaints can be filed anonymously on cybercrime.gov.in.
3. Right to fund recovery: The bank is obligated to acknowledge your fraud complaint and investigate within a fixed timeline under RBI guidelines.
4. Right to compensation: Under IT Act Section 43, victims can claim compensation from the offending party through the Adjudicating Officer.

RBI GUIDELINES FOR ZERO LIABILITY

Under RBI circular on Limiting Liability of Customers in Unauthorized Electronic Transactions:
- If fraud occurs WITHOUT your negligence (e.g., phishing by hacker, SIM swap): You have ZERO liability. Bank must reverse full amount within 10 working days.
- If fraud occurs WITH your negligence (you shared OTP): Liability is shared, but partial reversal may still be possible depending on your bank.
- Report to bank WITHIN 3 days for full zero-liability coverage.
- Timeline: Bank must acknowledge complaint within 3 working days and resolve within 90 days.

KEY GOVERNMENT BODIES

I4C — Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre: nodal body under MHA that coordinates all cyber crime response across states. They operate the 1930 helpline.
CERT-In — Indian Computer Emergency Response Team: handles cybersecurity incidents, breaches, and vulnerabilities. Toll-free: 1800-11-0001.
NPCI — National Payments Corporation of India: handles UPI, IMPS, and digital payment disputes.
TRAI — Telecom Regulatory Authority of India: handles SIM fraud, unsolicited calls, and spam. Report via DND app or 1909.
UIDAI — Unique Identification Authority of India: handles Aadhaar misuse. Helpline: 1947.
