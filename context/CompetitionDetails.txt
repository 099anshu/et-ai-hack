All that you need to know about ET AI Hackathon 2.0
Note: The Phase 1 results will be announced on 22nd June before 3:00 PM

The ET AI Hackathon 2.0, in partnership with Unstop, is a nation-scale innovation challenge designed to discover and celebrate India’s brightest emerging AI talent. Building on the success of its first edition, this flagship platform brings together the country’s sharpest minds to solve real-world AI problem statements across business innovation, social impact, and open innovation domains.

Across a multi-phase journey, beginning with an online AI assessment, advancing into an intensive prototype build sprint, and culminating in a high-energy grand finale, participants ideate, innovate, and present transformative AI solutions.

ET AI Hackathon 2.0 is where ideas evolve into impactful products, and where the next generation of AI leaders takes shape.

Read more details - https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/et-ai-hackathon/2nd-edition

Eligibility Criteria:
Open to students, professionals, and AI enthusiasts across India.
Participation allowed individually or in teams of 1-4 members
Cross-college, cross-discipline, and cross-year teams are allowed
Ideal for developers, designers, and product thinkers building AI solutions
Each participant can be part of only one team
Rules and Guidelines:
Team formation must be completed before the Phase 1 assessment
No changes to team members will be allowed after the deadline
All ideas, code, documents, and assets must be original and created during the hackathon
Plagiarism, use of pre-built projects, or unauthorized third-party material will lead to disqualification
All submissions must be made via the Unstop platform within the given timelines
Licensed tools/datasets may be used only with valid authorization
Participants must ensure all submission links (GitHub, demo, docs) are public and accessible
Participants are expected to maintain professional and ethical conduct throughout the hackathon
Judging Criteria:
Prototypes will be evaluated based on:

Relevance to Problem Statement – How effectively the solution addresses the chosen problem
Innovation & Creativity – Originality and problem-solving approach
Technical Implementation – Quality of architecture, code, and use of AI
Business Viability – Practicality and real-world applicability
Presentation & Clarity – Effectiveness of demo, pitch, and communication
Impact & Scalability – Potential to create meaningful impact at scale
Final Authority:
All decisions made by the jury will be final and binding
The organisers reserve the right to modify rules, guidelines, or timelines if required

Your Hackathon Journey:
Register on Unstop - Sign up and secure your spot. 
Form your team - Build a team of 1-4 members with complementary skills.
Complete the MCQ assessment - Attempt an AI-proctored assessment to test your foundational knowledge.
Get shortlisted for Phase 2 - Qualified teams are shortlisted based on MCQ scores and notified on the platform.
Pick your problem statement - Choose from real-world AI challenges revealed at the start of Phase 2.
Build & submit your prototype - Develop your AI solution and submit via Unstop before the deadline.
Final shortlist announced - Top submissions are evaluated and shortlisted for the Grand Finale.
Present at the Grand Finale - Pitch your solution to an industry jury and compete for ₹10 Lakh+ in prizes.
